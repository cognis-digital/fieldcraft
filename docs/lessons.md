# Lessons from recent & ongoing conflicts (analytical / defensive)

> **Disclaimer:** Educational/analytical/defensive material. Not operational instructions for violence or weapons.

Distilled, individual-focused takeaways in the spirit of RUSI / West Point MWI / CSIS lessons-learned analysis. The emphasis is on what changed and how to PROTECT, treat, sustain, and endure — not how to attack.

## drones_uas
- **L-UAS-01 (Ukraine 2022-2026, Nagorno-Karabakh 2020):** Cheap small UAS and FPV/loitering munitions now saturate the battlefield; almost anything seen can be struck, and the front is transparent to overhead view.
  - *Takeaway:* Assume you are observed from above at all times. Disperse, minimize time in the open, use overhead cover and terrain, and control thermal/visual/acoustic signature.

## counter_uas
- **L-UAS-02 (Ukraine 2022-2026):** Detecting and countering small drones is hard; individual awareness and passive protection matter as much as any active counter-measure.
  - *Takeaway:* Learn drone sound/sight cues, keep overhead cover within a few seconds' reach, spread out (don't cluster), and harden fighting/rest positions with overhead protection.

## ew_comms
- **L-EW-01 (Ukraine 2022-2026):** Pervasive electronic warfare degrades GPS and radios; emissions get located and targeted.
  - *Takeaway:* Keep a real PACE comms plan, train land navigation WITHOUT GPS (map/compass/terrain), minimize transmissions, and practice emission discipline.

## isr_signature
- **L-ISR-01 (Ukraine, Gaza):** Ubiquitous ISR (drones, thermal, satellites) makes hiding the central survival problem.
  - *Takeaway:* Manage signature across spectra: shape/shine/shadow/silhouette/movement + thermal. Move at limited visibility, avoid patterns, and keep heat sources concealed.

## combat_medicine
- **L-MED-01 (Ukraine 2022-2026):** Evacuation is often delayed for hours/days because drones threaten the routes, forcing Prolonged Field Care far beyond the classic 'golden hour'.
  - *Takeaway:* Train beyond immediate TCCC: hemorrhage control PLUS prolonged care — hypothermia prevention, tourniquet conversion, hydration, documentation, casualty sheltering.

## logistics
- **L-LOG-01 (Ukraine 2022-2026):** Resupply, batteries, and casualty movement are all harder under precision/drone threat; stockpiles must be dispersed and power is a constant constraint.
  - *Takeaway:* Plan power (batteries/solar) and water/food redundancy; disperse caches; assume resupply may be delayed; keep an individual sustainment load.

## small_unit
- **L-SU-01 (Ukraine 2022-2026):** Forces have dispersed, decentralized, and re-learned field fortification; massed or static positions are quickly found and struck.
  - *Takeaway:* Small, dispersed, dug-in/covered, and self-reliant survives; build protective positions, avoid clustering, and be able to operate with minimal external support.

## resilience
- **L-RES-01 (Ukraine 2022-2026):** Constant drone observation and sustained operations impose severe psychological load.
  - *Takeaway:* Build mental resilience and sustainment habits: sleep discipline when possible, routine, small-team cohesion, and deliberate recovery; manage fear with training and competence.
