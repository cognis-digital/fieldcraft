# CBRN protective basics (defensive)

> **Disclaimer:** Defensive, protective, life-saving awareness only — how to recognize
> a chemical/biological/radiological/nuclear hazard and protect yourself. This page
> contains **nothing** about producing, weaponizing, or employing any such agent. If
> you suspect a real CBRN event, move away, get to clean air/shelter, and contact
> emergency services.

CBRN = **C**hemical, **B**iological, **R**adiological, **N**uclear hazards. Civilians
and soldiers alike benefit from basic recognition and immediate protective actions —
the goal here is *protection and survival*, the same material taught in public
preparedness and first-responder awareness courses.

## Recognize — indicators of a hazard

- Many casualties with similar symptoms in one area; dead birds/insects/animals.
- Unexplained odors, mists, oily droplets, or colored vapor; dispersal devices.
- Sudden eye/skin/respiratory irritation, excessive secretions, or unexplained illness.
- Official alerts, sirens, or a recognized attack/industrial release.

## Immediate protective actions (the priorities)

1. **Avoid / move** — distance and *upwind/uphill* of the source is your best
   protection. Get out of the affected area quickly and calmly.
2. **Protect breathing** — cover nose/mouth; a fitted protective mask is best, but
   even moving to clean air and avoiding inhalation matters most.
3. **Shelter** — for many releases, going inside, closing up, and "shelter in place"
   per official guidance protects better than moving through a cloud.
4. **Cover skin** — minimize skin exposure to droplets/dust.
5. **Decontaminate** — remove contaminated clothing (cutting it off avoids pulling it
   over the head); rinse skin with copious water/soap; don't spread it.
6. **Get help** — seek medical care and follow official instructions.

## Concepts you'll hear (descriptive)

- **MOPP** (Mission-Oriented Protective Posture) — the military framework of escalating
  protective-equipment levels (mask, then suit/gloves/boots) matched to the threat.
- **Protective mask** — filters airborne agents; fit and seal are everything.
- **Decontamination** — physically removing/neutralizing the hazard; time + distance +
  removing contaminated layers are the core ideas.
- **Radiological**: minimize **time** near the source, maximize **distance**, use
  **shielding** (mass between you and the source) — the "time/distance/shielding" rule.

## Preparedness

Know your area's industrial and natural hazards; keep N95/respiratory protection,
water, and plastic sheeting/tape in your kit; follow FEMA/Ready.gov and local guidance.
